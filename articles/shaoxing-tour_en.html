Two Days in Shaoxing: Exploring a Literary and Cultural Gem

October 25, 2023 | By William Thompson

Shaoxing, often overlooked by international travelers, is one of China's most historically significant cities with over 2,500 years of rich cultural heritage. Once the capital of the ancient Yue Kingdom, this enchanting water town in Zhejiang Province has nurtured some of China's most celebrated literary figures, including Lu Xun, Wang Xizhi, and Cai Yuanpei. With its network of canals, distinctive black-awning boats, and well-preserved ancient architecture, Shaoxing offers visitors an authentic glimpse into traditional Jiangnan (south of the Yangtze River) culture. Here's a carefully crafted two-day itinerary to help you discover the best this literary city has to offer.

Day One: Literary Heritage and Romantic Tales

Morning: Lu Xun's Former Residence

Begin your Shaoxing adventure around 8:00 AM at Lu Xun's Former Residence, the childhood home of China's most influential modern writer. This well-preserved complex includes several interconnected courtyards and buildings where Lu Xun (born Zhou Shuren) spent his formative years. The site encompasses the Old and New Family Houses, Hundred Herbs Garden, Three-Flavor Study Room, and the Lu Xun Memorial Hall.

Pay special attention to the Three-Flavor Study Room (San Wei Shu Wu), the private school where young Lu Xun received his classical education. The room maintains its original furnishings, including the large character for "early" hanging on the wall—a reminder for students to rise early for their studies. This location inspired Lu Xun's famous essay "From the Hundred Herbs Garden to the Three-Flavor Study Room," offering insights into traditional Chinese education.

While in the area, don't miss the opportunity to take a short ride on a traditional black-awning boat (wupeng chuan). These distinctive vessels have been Shaoxing's primary mode of water transportation for centuries. As your boatman poles through the narrow waterways, you'll gain a unique perspective of the ancient water town architecture and daily life along the canals.

Traveler's Tip

Lu Xun's Former Residence is free to visit, but requires advance reservation through the "Shaoxing Tourism" WeChat official account. Arriving early in the morning helps you avoid tour groups and provides a more contemplative atmosphere to appreciate the historical significance of the site.

Lunch: Authentic Shaoxing Cuisine

For lunch, head to Xunbao Ji Shaoxing Restaurant (on Lu Xun Road), a local establishment serving traditional Shaoxing dishes in an elegant setting. The menu features several specialties worth trying:

  • Yellow Wine Drunken Chicken (huang jiu zui ji): Tender chicken marinated in Shaoxing's famous yellow rice wine, creating a delicate flavor with subtle wine aromatics
  • Yellow Wine Zongzi: Glutinous rice dumplings infused with Shaoxing wine, offering a unique twist on this traditional food
  • Stinky Tofu (chou doufu): Shaoxing's version is less pungent than other regional varieties, with a crispy exterior and soft interior
  • Fennel Seeds with Yellow Wine (hui xiang dou): A perfect accompaniment to your meal, these beans are stir-fried with fennel seeds and Shaoxing wine

Complement your meal with a pot of warm Shaoxing yellow wine (huang jiu), served in the traditional manner in small porcelain cups. This amber-colored rice wine, with a history spanning over 2,500 years, is integral to Shaoxing's cultural identity.

Afternoon: Shen Garden

After lunch, make your way to Shen Garden (Shen Yuan), a classical Chinese garden dating back to the Southern Song Dynasty (1127-1279). This intimate garden gained fame as the setting for one of China's most poignant love stories—the chance meeting between the poet Lu You and his former wife Tang Wan, which inspired Lu's renowned poem "Phoenix Hairpin" (Chai Tou Feng).

Key features of the garden include:

  • Lu You Memorial Hall: Displaying artifacts and information about the poet's life and literary achievements
  • Phoenix Hairpin Pavilion: The legendary meeting place of Lu You and Tang Wan, with the famous poem inscribed inside
  • Canglang Pavilion: Offering panoramic views of the garden's landscape
  • Plum Blossom Hall: Showcasing exquisite garden architecture

If your timing allows, attend the Phoenix Hairpin Poetry Recital at 3:00 PM, where performers in period costumes bring the tragic love story to life through traditional Chinese performance arts.

Evening: Cangqiao Straight Street

As evening approaches, take a leisurely stroll along Cangqiao Straight Street (Cangqiao Zhi Jie), a well-preserved ancient street flanked by traditional buildings and intersected by picturesque canals. The street comes alive at night with lanterns reflecting on the water, creating a magical atmosphere that captures the essence of old Shaoxing.

This is the perfect place to sample local street food specialties:

  • Wang Laohan's Stinky Tofu: A famous local stall serving what many consider the best stinky tofu in town
  • Gao Laotai's Cream Pastries: Delicate pastries with a creamy filling, made using traditional methods
  • Drunken Crabs: Fresh river crabs marinated in Shaoxing wine
  • Mei Gan Cai Kou Rou: Pork belly with preserved vegetables, a hearty local favorite

The street also houses numerous shops selling Shaoxing yellow wine, handcrafted souvenirs, and local art, perfect for picking up mementos of your visit.

Day Two: Cultural Heritage and Ancient Wisdom

Morning: Shaoxing Museum

Begin your second day at the Shaoxing Museum, the largest comprehensive museum in the region. Housing over 13,000 artifacts, the museum offers an excellent overview of Shaoxing's 2,500-year history, from the ancient Yue Kingdom to modern times.

The museum is organized into several thematic exhibitions:

  • Yue Kingdom History: Featuring bronze weapons, including the famous Yue Kingdom swords known for their exceptional craftsmanship
  • Calligraphy Art: Highlighting Shaoxing's reputation as the "City of Calligraphy" with works by Wang Xizhi and other masters
  • Yellow Wine Culture: Documenting the history and production techniques of Shaoxing's famous wine
  • Water Town Folk Customs: Displaying traditional tools, furniture, and everyday items that illustrate life in ancient Shaoxing

Visitor Information

The Shaoxing Museum is closed on Mondays. Opening hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (last entry at 4:30 PM). Admission is free, but advance reservation through the museum's official website or WeChat account is required. Guided tours in English are available at scheduled times and provide valuable context to the exhibits.

Lunch: Local Delicacies

For lunch, try Tongxin Lou Restaurant (on Liberation North Road) near the Calligraphy Saint's Former Residence. This established eatery serves traditional Shaoxing cuisine in a charming setting. Recommended dishes include:

  • Shaoxing Shengjianbao: Pan-fried dumplings with a crispy bottom and juicy filling
  • Mei Gan Cai Rou: Slow-cooked pork with preserved vegetables
  • Three-Flavor Stinky Tofu: Served with three different dipping sauces
  • Drunken Pork Ribs: Tender ribs marinated in Shaoxing wine

Afternoon: Calligraphy Saint's Former Residence

After lunch, visit the Calligraphy Saint's Former Residence (Shu Sheng Gu Li), dedicated to Wang Xizhi, widely regarded as China's greatest calligrapher. This tranquil complex is also the birthplace of Cai Yuanpei, a prominent educator and reformer in modern Chinese history.

Key attractions within the site include:

  • Cai Yuanpei's Former Residence: A well-preserved traditional home showcasing the living environment of this influential figure
  • Jiezhu Temple: A Tang Dynasty temple where Wang Xizhi often practiced his calligraphy
  • Wang Xizhi Exhibition Hall: Displaying reproductions of his most famous works and explaining his profound influence on Chinese calligraphy
  • Tieshan Bridge: A picturesque stone bridge where Wang Xizhi reportedly wrote inscriptions for fans, making it a popular photo spot

Don't miss the Old Gentleman's Bookstore within the complex, where you can purchase hand-written bookmarks created by local calligraphy enthusiasts. These personalized souvenirs make meaningful mementos of your visit to the home of Chinese calligraphy.

Practical Information

Admission Fees

Lu Xun's Former Residence is free, but requires advance reservation. Shen Garden costs 40 yuan for daytime admission.

For visitors planning to see multiple attractions, the Shaoxing Ancient City Pass (choose 4 out of 5 sites) offers good value at 145 yuan when purchased in advance. It includes Shen Garden (daytime), East Lake Scenic Area, Da Yu Mausoleum, Lan Ting Scenic Area, and Yangming's Former Residence.

Transportation

From Hangzhou, take Hangzhou Metro Line 1 directly to "Lu Xun's Former Residence Station" to reach downtown Shaoxing—a convenient option that takes approximately 30 minutes.

Within Shaoxing, most attractions are relatively close to each other. Transportation options include:

  • Walking: Many sites are within walking distance of each other, allowing you to absorb the city's atmosphere
  • Shared Bicycles: Widely available throughout the city center
  • Public Buses: Shaoxing has an extensive bus network with fares of 1-2 yuan, payable via Alipay or WeChat
  • Taxis: Starting fare is 8 yuan, with most in-city trips costing less than 30 yuan

Accommodation Recommendations

  • Shaoxing Lanting Pushi Hotel: Located at 27 Yangming Road, this hotel features Hui-style architecture and elegant surroundings, ideal for travelers seeking high-quality accommodation.
  • Shaoxing Oriental Mountain Water Golden Sand Hotel: Situated at 88 Jishan South Road, this hotel offers spacious rooms with views of the amusement park, making it perfect for families traveling with children.
  • Shaoxing Ancient City Inn: Near Lu Xun's Former Residence, this boutique hotel is housed in a traditional water town building, offering moderate prices and an authentic local atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit

Shaoxing is pleasant to visit year-round, but spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer the most comfortable weather for exploring the city on foot, with mild temperatures and less rainfall.

If you visit in mid-November, you might catch the annual Shaoxing Yellow Wine Festival, when you can tour wine-making facilities, sample various yellow wines, and enjoy cultural performances celebrating this important aspect of local heritage.

Conclusion

Shaoxing offers visitors a rare opportunity to step back in time and experience the literary and cultural heritage that has shaped Chinese civilization for millennia. While two days cannot encompass all of Shaoxing's treasures, this itinerary provides a balanced introduction to the city's most significant historical sites, culinary delights, and cultural experiences.

Whether you're tracing the footsteps of literary giants, savoring the subtle flavors of Shaoxing cuisine, or simply absorbing the timeless atmosphere of its ancient waterways, Shaoxing rewards travelers with authentic experiences far removed from the tourist crowds of China's more famous destinations.

We hope this guide helps you plan a memorable journey to one of China's most culturally significant yet underappreciated cities.
