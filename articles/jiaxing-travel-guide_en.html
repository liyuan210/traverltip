Introduction: Where History Meets Tradition

Jiaxing, located in northern Zhejiang Province, holds a unique position in both Chinese history and culture. This ancient city is most famous as the birthplace of the Chinese Communist Party, where the First National Congress concluded on a red boat in South Lake in 1921. Beyond its revolutionary significance, Jiaxing is renowned for its traditional water town charm, delicious zongzi (rice dumplings), and well-preserved ancient architecture.

Historical Landmarks & Revolutionary Sites

South Lake (Nanhu) & The Red Boat

South Lake is the most significant historical site in Jiaxing, where the First National Congress of the Chinese Communist Party concluded in July 1921. The replica of the famous "Red Boat" serves as a powerful symbol of the party's founding and China's revolutionary history.

Historical Significance

The Red Boat on South Lake represents the birthplace of the Chinese Communist Party. In 1921, delegates moved their meeting from Shanghai to this boat to avoid police surveillance, making crucial decisions that would shape China's future.

South Lake Revolutionary Memorial Hall

This comprehensive museum showcases the history of the Chinese Communist Party's founding, featuring original documents, photographs, and artifacts from the revolutionary period. The exhibition provides detailed context about the political and social conditions of early 20th century China.

Yanyu Tower (Misty Rain Tower)

This ancient tower, originally built during the Five Dynasties period (907-960), offers panoramic views of South Lake. The tower has been rebuilt several times throughout history and serves as a symbol of Jiaxing's cultural heritage.

Ancient Water Towns & Cultural Sites

Xitang Ancient Town

Located about 30 minutes from Jiaxing city center, Xitang is one of China's most well-preserved ancient water towns. With over 1,000 years of history, it features covered corridors, stone bridges, and traditional architecture along peaceful waterways.

Best Photography Spots

Xitang is famous for its covered corridors and reflection shots. The best times for photography are early morning (6-8 AM) and late afternoon (4-6 PM) when the light is soft and the crowds are smaller.

Wuzhen Water Town

Another famous water town near Jiaxing, Wuzhen is divided into East and West scenic areas. The town showcases traditional Chinese architecture, folk customs, and cultural performances, making it a living museum of Jiangnan culture.

Culinary Heritage: The Home of Zongzi

ü•ü Famous Jiaxing Specialties

Jiaxing is renowned throughout China for its culinary traditions, particularly the famous Jiaxing zongzi (rice dumplings). The city's location in the fertile Yangtze River Delta has blessed it with abundant ingredients and a rich food culture.

Jiaxing Zongzi

The most famous variety features glutinous rice wrapped with pork belly and salted egg yolk, creating a perfect balance of flavors. Wufangzhai is the most famous brand.

Nanhu Water Chestnuts

Sweet and crispy water chestnuts grown in South Lake, traditionally enjoyed fresh or used in desserts and stir-fries.

Jiaxing Rice Wine

Local rice wine with a mild, sweet flavor, often served warm and paired with traditional dishes.

Wenhu Crab

Freshwater crabs from local lakes, best enjoyed during autumn when they're at their most flavorful.

Traditional Noodle Soup

Hand-pulled noodles in clear broth with local vegetables and meat, a comfort food staple.

Osmanthus Cake

Delicate sweet cake flavored with osmanthus flowers, a traditional dessert especially popular in autumn.

Zongzi Cultural Experience

Visit the Wufangzhai Museum to learn about the 100-year history of Jiaxing zongzi making. Some locations offer hands-on workshops where you can learn to wrap your own zongzi using traditional techniques.

üí° Insider Tips

  • Revolutionary History: Book guided tours for deeper historical context of the Red Boat and Communist Party founding
  • Water Towns: Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and get better photos
  • Food Experience: Try authentic Jiaxing zongzi from local shops, not just tourist areas
